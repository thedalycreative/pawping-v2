Logo System

Primary Logo

A stylised, geometric paw print with angular shapes and a balanced earthy palette. Designed to feel professional, trustworthy, and outdoors‑oriented rather than cutesy or childish.

  • Use on website headers, signage, business cards, and official documents.
  • Maintain clear space equal to at least the height of one toe pad around the logo.
  • Avoid placing on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid or translucent overlay.

Icon & Monogram Usage

The paw icon alone can be used as a social avatar, app icon, or small‑scale mark.

  • Use icon‑only at small sizes where text would be unreadable.
  • For monochrome applications, use solid dark wood or deep ocean blue.
  • Keep angles and proportions consistent—do not stretch or distort the paw.

Color Palette

A balanced earthy mix that reflects nature, reliability, and warmth. Use color intentionally—keep layouts mostly neutral with one or two accent colors.

Color Usage Guidelines

  • Use Soft Neutral or white as the main background for readability.
  • Reserve Sunlight Gold for buttons, key highlights, and important information.
  • Use Grass Green and Ocean Blue as accent blocks, headings, or icon fills.
  • Keep overall designs to 2–3 colors at a time to maintain a clean, premium feel.

Typography

Typography is modern, clean, and slightly geometric to match the angular paw mark while staying warm and approachable.

Font Stack

Logo & Headings: Montserrat (Google Font)

Subheadings & Body: Lato (Google Font)

Accent / Quotes: Playfair Display Italic (optional)

Typography Guidelines

  • Use Montserrat for headings, navigation, and key labels.
  • Use Lato for paragraphs, service descriptions, and small UI text.
  • Use Playfair Display Italic sparingly for quotes or emotional emphasis.
  • Maintain generous line spacing and avoid all‑caps in body text for readability.

Brand Personality & Voice

Personality

Professional Trustworthy Outdoorsy Warm Calm

The brand should feel like a reliable, experienced friend who genuinely cares about each dog’s wellbeing. It’s not overly cute or chaotic—more grounded, capable, and nature‑aligned.

Voice & Messaging

  • Voice: Clear, warm, confident, and reassuring.
  • Avoid: Baby talk, excessive exclamation marks, or overly corporate jargon.
  • Focus on: Safety, reliability, outdoor time, and peace of mind for owners.

Example micro‑copy:

“Every walk, every stay, every play session is designed to keep your dog happy, safe, and fulfilled.”
